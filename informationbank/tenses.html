Popular and More Frequently usable Sentences:

* Future perfect: Composed of two elements the simple future of the verb "to have" (will have) + the past participle of the main verb.
When to Use:
The future perfect tense refers to a completed action in the future. When we use this tense we are projecting ourselves forward into the future and looking back at an action that will be completed some time later than now. It is most often used with a time expression.
Examples
  • I will have been here for six months on June 23rd.
  • By the time you read this I will have left.
  • You will have finished your report by this time next week.
  • Won't they have arrived by 5:00?
  • Will you have eaten when I pick you up?

Bought Vs Brought:
        ‘Bought’ is the past tense of ‘buy’: I bought a new car last week.
        ‘Brought’ is the past tense of ‘bring’: I brought him a glass of water.

        He was born and brought up in India.

Suit Vs Suite: ‘Suit’ is a noun which means “set of something, maybe clothes, cards, jewellery, etc”.
    - The suit looks perfect on you.

    ‘Suite’ is a noun which means “a connection series of rooms to be used together”
    - The Presidential suite of this hotel is very luxurious.

Showing Possession:

  • In English, possession can be shown by using possessive nouns and pronouns.
  • I went to my parents's house. (Incorrect)
  • I went to my parents' house. (Correct)
  • This book is her. (Incorrect)
  • This book is hers. (Correct)


Comparatives:
comparative form of an adjective or adverb can be formed by adding the suffix -er or by placing more (or less) before.

He can run more faster. (Incorrect)
She was more prettier. (Incorrect)
David was less smarter than John. (Incorrect)
He is more taller than me. (Incorrect)
He is taller than me. (Correct)

Be careful not to form a so-called double comparative (e.g., by adding -er and using more / less ). This is a grammatical howler. (It is more common in speech than in writing.)

Examples:
Example    Word Type   Comparative
small           adjective        smaller
quickly        adverb           more quickly
quickly        adverb           less quickly
