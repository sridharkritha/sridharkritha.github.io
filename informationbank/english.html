A. When to add “s” to a Noun and Verb:

Noun: Noun plurals are formed by adding “s.”
    singular - Button , Card
    plurals  - Buttons, Cards
Verb: verb plurals are formed by removing “s.”
    singular - Talks, gets, goes
    plurals  - Talk , get , go

Examples:

1. He talks (Singular Verb)
2. They talk (plural Verb )
3. She goes/go away every August.  (Ans: goes - Singular)
4. When he gets/get angry, his face turns red. (Ans: gets - singular)
5. When he and Jenny walks/walk to work, they hold hands. (Ans: walk -plural)
6. It makes our life easy.

B. When to use - has/have + been + present participle:
 [Present Perfect Continuous]:

Something started in the past and has continued up until now.

Example:
1. They have been talking for the last hour.
2. She has been working at that company for three years.
3. Recently, I have been feeling really tired.

It is important to remember that Non-Continuous Verbs cannot be used in any continuous tenses - Instead ou must use Present Perfect.

Examples:
1. Sam has been having his car for two years. (Not Correct)
    Sam has had his car for two years.         (Correct)

Cheat Sheet for the Usage of There, Their, and They’re

 

When to Use

How to Test

There

Naming a place, a thing, or the existence of something

Replace “there” with “here”

Their

Showing possession

Replace “their” with “our”

They’re

Combining the words “they” and “are”

Replace “they’re” with “they are”

 

Examples:

There:

1. “I’ve never been there before.”

 Test: “I’ve never been here before.”

 (Correct!)

2. “This is there ball of green string.”

 Test: “This is here ball of green string.”

 (Incorrect.)

Their:

 1. “I went to their cottage last summer.”

 Test: “I went to our cottage last summer.”

 (Correct!)

2. “Their is the pizza place I was telling you about!”

 Test: “Our is the pizza place I was telling you about!”

 (Incorrect.)

They’re:

1. “They’re the two best players in the league.”

 Test: They are the two best players in the league.”

 (Correct!)

2. “My cousin is one of they’re closest friends.”

 Test: “My cousin is one of they are closest friends.”

 (Incorrect.)


Notes:

They're is the contracted form of They are.
There is used as an introductory subject.
 Examples:
There are many people in that room.
That's my house over there.

Test your usage. When you use any of these three words, get in the habit of asking yourself these questions, but remember that they will not work in all cases though:

    If you wrote there, will the sentence still make sense if you replace it with here? If so, you're using it correctly.
    If you chose their, will the sentence still make sense if you replace it with our? If so, you've chosen the correct word.
    If you used they're, will the sentence still make sense if you replace it with they are? If so, you're on the right track!


Bought Vs Brought:
Both words are used when communicating in the past tense, but have entirely different meanings.

Bought: past tense of "to buy".
"Jeremy bought a new computer for £500."

Brought: past tense of "to bring".
"Katherine brought several bottles of wine to the party."

1. All the document has been uploaded on sever.
2. He has uploaded all the document on sever.

Ans:
1) All the documents have been uploaded on the server.
2) He has uploaded all the documents on the server.

"Did you find?" and "Have you found?"

Ans:

Did you find is the simple past and indicates that you are no longer looking for it.

Have you found is the present perfect and indicates a link with the present, specifically, that you could still be looking for it.
